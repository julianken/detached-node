### Strategic Findings
- **Saturation paradox resolved:** "Multi-agent orchestration" is enterprise-saturated; practitioners search different terms ("git worktree agents", "sub-agent coordination", "agentic workflow debugging"). Wedge keywords are the path.
- **Novelty validation:** 2 of 4 "novel" concepts confirmed (node-locality, overlapping lanes of fire); 2 downgraded to "novel framing with closing window" (context pollution, bounded context dispatch). Compound philosophy ("4 ways AI collapses into self-confirming loops") has no published equivalent.
- **Emotional hooks:** Entry emotion varies by concept (frustration, FOMO, anxiety, guilt); retention driver is consistently aspiration. Key differentiator: practitioner voice names uncertainty precisely instead of resolving it with false confidence.
- **Content architecture:** Pillar page ("Multi-Agent Orchestration: Practitioner's Field Guide") + 8 cluster articles + 3 supporting pieces. 8-week publication schedule. Pillar/cluster model shows 63% more rankings within 90 days.
- **Format differentiation:** Annotated config blocks (zero competitors), failure mode diagrams (zero competitors), cost breakdowns with real numbers (nearly zero competitors).

## Confidence Levels
- **High:** Blog post ranking, publication order, wedge keyword strategy, format differentiation via annotated configs, emotional hook mapping
- **Medium:** Novelty window timing (6-12 months for context pollution, longer for node-locality), cost optimization keyword growth trajectory
- **Low:** Specific search volume numbers (estimates from indirect signals), long-term durability of worktree approach
