# Context Packet: Phase 3

## Synthesis Outputs Summary

### Synthesis 1 (Thematic): "The Eight Ideas Are One Argument"
- The 8 blog ideas are not independent — they are 8 proofs of a single thesis: "the failure modes of AI-assisted development are structural, predictable, and nameable"
- Three themes: (A) The Isolation Problem (spatial + temporal), (B) The Signal Problem (self-corruption, verification, hygiene), (C) The Architecture Problem (economic, probabilistic, spatial)
- Themes have dependency: Isolation → Signal → Architecture (you can't fix signal without isolation, can't reason about architecture without both)
- Proposed narrative publication sequence differs from SEO-optimized sequence — recommends publishing narratively but promoting via SEO sequence
- Writing order: write #5 (Probability-Aware) first as capstone, then #1 (Git Worktrees), then #2 (Echo Chamber)
- Editorial identity: "We name the failure modes of AI-assisted development so you can design around them"
- Cross-article vocabulary table defined (8 terms that must be consistent)

### Synthesis 2 (Risk/Opportunity): "What Can Go Wrong and When"
- Compaction-sycophancy window revised DOWN to 3-6 months (not 6-12). Amp, Jason Liu, MIT all actively in motion
- Git worktree article has medium-high tooling supersession risk (6-18 months). Must be framed architecturally, not procedurally
- Opus/Sonnet cost breakdown has high model-name decay risk. Write as methodology, not specific numbers
- New blog E-E-A-T problem is real — first 3 articles need unusually strong first-hand evidence
- Anthropic incumbent problem: HIGH severity for context pollution, epistemic discipline. LOW for node-locality, overlapping lanes
- "AI slop" risk is CRITICAL for probability-aware piece and ELEVATED for echo chamber piece
- 5 content risks, 5 execution risks, 5 strategic risks all mapped with specific mitigations
- Recommended 3 fast-publish items: watershed moment piece (60 days max), compaction-sycophancy (45 days), node-locality vocabulary (monitor for overnight collapse)

### Synthesis 3 (Gaps & Implications): "What We Don't Know and Who We're Missing"
- 6 evidence gaps identified: emotional driver percentages apply to wrong population, keyword volumes are directional not absolute, novelty windows have no monitoring, single-source concept derivation, DORA data needs verification, no competitive early-warning system
- 3 missing audience segments: engineering team leads (highest backlink value), non-Claude-Code users (Cursor/Copilot practitioners), academic/researcher crossover
- Blog identity implications: Detached Node must be a "naming publication" — its value is giving practitioners vocabulary for their experience
- GitHub repo of working configs is the single highest-leverage trust signal
- Monitoring cadence needed: ~1 hour/week across RSS feeds, HN, arXiv for window collapse detection

## Areas of Agreement (all 3 syntheses)
- Git worktrees article should publish first (but framed as isolation principle, not how-to)
- Node-locality and overlapping lanes of fire are the highest-novelty vocabulary plays
- Probability-aware design is the philosophical capstone and most durable piece
- Practitioner voice + specificity is the primary differentiator; losing it produces AI slop
- Time pressure is real for compaction-sycophancy and watershed moment content
- E-E-A-T through vocabulary origination, not authority borrowing

## Areas of Divergence
- Publication sequence: Synthesis 1 wants narrative arc (concrete → conceptual → abstract), Synthesis 2 wants urgency-driven (publish closing-window pieces first), Synthesis 3 wants evidence-gathering before publishing novel concepts
- Risk tolerance: Synthesis 2 treats novelty windows as urgent; Synthesis 3 notes single-source derivation risk and recommends validation before publishing
- Scope: Synthesis 1 is pure content strategy; Synthesis 3 argues the blog needs infrastructure (monitoring, GitHub repo, author identity) before content strategy matters
