# Phase 0: Analysis Brief — Blog Content Ideation from Sub-Agent Orchestration Transcript

## Analysis Question

What are the most valuable, differentiated, and SEO-discoverable blog post ideas that can be extracted from a practitioner's voice transcript about sub-agent orchestration patterns for AI coding tools?

Restated in bullets:
- The transcript captures real hands-on experience with multi-agent AI coding workflows (not theory)
- Core concepts: context isolation, parallel specialist dispatch, hallucination mitigation via redundancy, git worktree isolation, iterative review waves, probability-aware AI workflow design
- The blog (Detached Node) targets technical practitioners using AI coding tools who want to level up
- We need ideas that are genuinely insightful — not generic "AI is great" content
- Each idea needs SEO grounding: keyword opportunity, search intent, competitive gap
- The ideas should be differentiated — things no one else is writing about yet, or angles that go deeper than existing content
- Content should feel like it comes from someone who actually does this, not someone summarizing docs
- The output is blog post ideas with SEO analysis, NOT the posts themselves

## Assumptions & Unknowns

### Known Knowns
- The author uses Claude Code with Opus orchestrator + Sonnet sub-agents
- The author uses Linear for issue tracking, git worktrees for isolation
- The transcript reflects real workflow patterns the author has implemented
- The blog is early-stage (Phase 1 scaffold), built with Next.js 16
- Target audience: technical practitioners using AI coding tools

### Known Unknowns
- Current search volume for sub-agent orchestration keywords (need SEO data)
- What competing content exists on these specific topics
- Which content formats perform best for this audience
- Whether the audience searches for these concepts by the names used in the transcript or by different terms
- How mature the SEO landscape is for AI coding workflow content (blue ocean vs. red ocean)

### Suspected Unknowns
- Whether there are adjacent keyword clusters we're not thinking of
- What content gaps exist between "vibe coding" content and "enterprise AI" content (the transcript lives in between)
- Whether the "probability-aware workflow" framing is novel enough to own as a content category

## Domain Tags
1. Architecture — agent orchestration patterns, context management, graph/node mental models
2. Developer Experience — workflow optimization, tooling UX, cost management
3. Content Strategy / SEO — keyword opportunities, search intent, competitive landscape
4. Audience Psychology — practitioner pain points, search behavior, maturity stages

## Quality Criteria

| Criterion | Weight | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Differentiation | 25% | Does the idea offer something genuinely unique vs. existing content? |
| SEO opportunity | 25% | Is there searchable demand? Can we rank? |
| Practitioner value | 20% | Would a real practitioner find this useful and actionable? |
| Depth potential | 15% | Can the idea sustain a substantive, non-shallow article? |
| Voice alignment | 15% | Does it fit the Detached Node editorial voice (analytical, not promotional)? |

## 5 Investigation Areas

### Area 1: SEO Landscape & Keyword Opportunity
Investigate the search landscape for concepts in the transcript. What terms have volume? What's the competitive density? Where are underserved keyword clusters? What related queries exist? Focus on long-tail opportunities that a new blog could realistically rank for.

### Area 2: Competitive Content Analysis
Survey existing content on sub-agent orchestration, AI coding workflows, context management for LLMs, and related topics. Where is existing content generic or shallow? What unique angles does this transcript provide that competitors miss? Identify content gaps.

### Area 3: Core Concept Extraction & Article Decomposition
Extract the discrete, teachable concepts from the transcript. How do they decompose into standalone articles vs. a series? What's the concept dependency graph? Which concepts are most novel? Which are most accessible as entry points?

### Area 4: Audience Intent & Pain Point Mapping
Map the transcript's concepts to real practitioner pain points. What problems are they trying to solve when they search? What stage of AI coding maturity are they at? How do pain points map to search intent (informational, navigational, transactional)?

### Area 5: Content Format & Structure Optimization
For each potential blog idea, what content format maximizes both discoverability and engagement? Tutorial vs. deep-dive vs. opinion vs. case study vs. comparison? What structural elements (diagrams, code examples, before/after) drive engagement for this audience?
